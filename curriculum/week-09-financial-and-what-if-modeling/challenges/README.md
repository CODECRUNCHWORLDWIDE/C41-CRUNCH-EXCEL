# Week 9 — Challenges

Two problems that push past the exercises. The first is a construction challenge — get the two-variable data table mechanics exactly right and a whole sensitivity grid falls out of one setup. The second is a modeling-and-judgment challenge — build three named, defensible scenarios and argue for what makes each one "Best," "Base," or "Worst" rather than just picking numbers that feel right.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Build a Two-Variable Sensitivity Table](challenge-01-sensitivity-table.md)** — see the loan payment across every combination of interest rate and loan term at once. *(~60–90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Model Best/Base/Worst Scenarios](challenge-02-three-scenario-model.md)** — build and defend three complete, named scenarios for Crunch's investment decision. *(~75–90 min.)*

## How these are judged

Neither challenge has partial credit for "it looks right" — a data table either recomputes correctly across the whole grid or it doesn't, and a scenario set either represents genuinely different, defensible assumptions or it's just three copies of the same guess with different labels. You're being judged on:

- **Correctness** — every cell in Challenge 1's grid matches what you'd get by manually changing the inputs and re-reading the formula; every scenario output in Challenge 2 traces back to its own stated assumptions.
- **Technique** — Challenge 1 specifically requires one data-table setup (Excel) or one filled formula pattern (Sheets) producing the whole grid, not 20 individually typed formulas.
- **Judgment, stated in writing** — Challenge 2 requires a one-sentence justification for *why* each scenario's numbers are plausible, not just three sets of numbers with labels attached.

Keep your work in the files described inside each challenge.
