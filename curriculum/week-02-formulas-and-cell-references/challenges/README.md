# Week 2 — Challenges

Two problems that push past the exercises. The first is a pure construction challenge — get the mixed-reference mechanics exactly right and a whole grid falls out of one formula. The second is a diagnostic challenge — a small workbook is handed to you already broken, and the skill being tested is reading formulas and error values to find and fix the bugs, not writing new ones from scratch.

1. **[Challenge 1 — One formula, whole grid](challenge-01-build-a-multiplication-grid.md)** — build a 12×12 multiplication table using a single formula filled in every direction. *(~45–60 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Repair a workbook of broken refs](challenge-02-fix-the-broken-references.md)** — six formulas, six different bugs, one at a time. Diagnose each from its symptom before fixing it. *(~60–75 min.)*

## How these are judged

Neither challenge has partial credit for "it looks right" — a spreadsheet is either computing correctly or it isn't, and you can verify that yourself before submitting. You're being judged on:

- **Correctness** — every cell computes the right value, verified against the spot checks given.
- **Technique** — Challenge 1 specifically requires one formula filled in two directions, not twelve formulas typed by hand; typing each cell individually defeats the point even if the numbers come out right.
- **Diagnosis, not guessing** — Challenge 2 asks you to state *what kind* of bug each broken formula has (wrong reference type, typo, wrong function, etc.) before you fix it. A correct fix with no stated diagnosis is half credit.

Keep your work in the challenge files described inside each challenge.
